Mitochondria

Introduction

The mitochondria is the powerhouse of the cell. The mitochondria is an organelle in all eukaryotic cells, like human skin cells or plant cells. The mitochrondria is used to perform cellular respiration, which converts nutrients into energy in the form of ATP.

Properties of the Mitochondria

  • double membrance
  • found in multicellular organisms
  • contains its own DNA

Cellular Respiration

Cellular respiration is the chemical process done in the mitochrondria of cells to convert nutrients into energy. This energy is produced in the form of ATP, or adenosine triphosphate. The nutrients needed to perform cellular respiration are carbohydrates and oxygen. Water, carbon dioxide, and ATP come out. Cellular respiration that requires oxygen is called aerobic respiration.
